Tseng Su-Fen

Here you'll see a video clip of Tseng Su-Fen of Taiwan bowling in the 1999 FIQ World Championships (Abu Dhabi). The spinner release is considerably different than the hook/heavy-ball delivery taught in the West. In the background you can see Ko Li Chen, another excellent spinner from Taiwan.

This video clip will take a few minutes to load: when it does you'll see a five-step approach including the pushaway, release and followthrough. Note that, in the helicopter/spinner style popular in Taiwan and asia, the bowler rotates the fingers around the top of the ball in the release: this makes the ball spin on a near-vertical axis and increases deflection as the ball hits the pins. Originally I wasn't going to post this video as I feared it might be dismissed by some. Yes, the timing differs greatly from westerners - are taught as 'proper' bowling. I did put it here, though, after a helicopter-style reader requested it. So, if you are not a spinner bowler, try not to to be judgemental. If the goal is to knock down more pins than your opponent consider that, until 1999, Su-Fen held the highest ladies average in the 30-year history of the AMF World Cup: a 220.3 for 48 games in 1997 (not aided by a walled-up league condition). The World Cup is an annual competition where countries from around the world, including such greats as USA, Sweden, Malaysia etc., send one male and one lady bowler to compete. In that event she scored more than every other woman and, incredibly, all but two of the men (one of whom was a fellow spinner from Taiwan!).

When I watch this clip I see perhaps one of the best examples of a loose free swing you can hope to get. There is nothing forced here. Use your mouse to play the final few frames in super-slow-motion and you'll see how she has developed a style which is optimised for this release: watch the trailing leg work in time with the shoulder for a consistent and effective delivery. It reminds me oddly of Walter Ray Williams: his style is different, too, but he's very successful.
