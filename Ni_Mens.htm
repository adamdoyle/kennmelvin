Gary Craig

based in the Sportsbowl, Ballymena
Gary won Gold and Silver medals in the 1998 Triple Crown (Dundonald, NI) and placed sixth in the Singles event in the 1997 Europeans (Nottingham, Eng.). He started 1999 well, finishing 20th in the Irish Open (210 entries) and played in the 1999 FIQ World Championships (UAE).
     
Top
   
 

Kenny Donnelly

based in the Sportsbowl, Ballymena
Kenny represented Northern Ireland in the 1998 Commonwealth Games (Malaysia), a two-time winner of the NI Open he played for NI in the 1995 FIQ's (Reno) and won medals in the 1996 and 1997 Triple Crowns. Kenny was in the men's team for the 1999 FIQ's (UAE).
     
Top
   
 

Kev Horton

based in the Sportsbowl, Ballymena
Kev represented Northern Ireland in the 1996 and 1998 AMF World Cups. He has one sanctioned 300 game and reached the masters section in both the 1997 Triple Crown (Scotland) and 1999 Triple Crown (Dublin) where he finished 3rd overall. He won the NI Open (98), played in the 95 FIQ's (Reno) and the 1999 FIQ World Championships (UAE).
     
Top
   
 

Paul McCauley

based in Brunswick Superbowl, Derry
Paul was the first bowler to score a perfect 300 game in an NI Ranking event. In the 1999 Triple Crown (Dublin) he won Silver medals in the Doubles and Fives and Bronze in the Trios. He competed for NI in the 1995 FIQ's (Reno) and, in 1998, finished top-10 in the Scottish, Leinster and NI Opens. Paul also played in the 1999 FIQ World Championships (UAE).
     

 

Top
   
 

Andy McLaren

based in County Donegal
Andy scored what we believe to be the first ever 300 in a Triple Crown (Seniors 1998, Dublin) and won Gold in the 1999 Seniors (Leeds). He played for NI in the 1995 FIQ's (Reno).
     
Top
   
 

Billy Nimick

based in Dundonald International Icebowl, Belfast
Billy was highest place NI Man in the 1999 British Nationals (England) making the final cut for the "masters" and finishing 12th in a entry of 215. He made the first-round cut in 1999 Israel Open, won Silver with the NI Men's Fives team in the 1997 Triple Crown (Scotland) and again the 1999 Triple Crown (Dublin). Billy was in the NI men's team for the 1999 FIQ's. In 2000 Billy won Gold in the Overall Men's events (Triple Crown, Leeds) scoring a perfect 300 game in the final game of match-play.

Martin Payne

based in Dundonald International Icebowl, Belfast
Martin won a Gold medal (Mens Doubles) in the 1998 Triple Crown (Dundonald, NI) and, in the 1999 Triple Crown (Dublin), won both Silver (Fives) and Bronze (Trios). Martin was in the mens team for the 1999 FIQ World Championships (UAE).

Gary Stoops

based in Dundonald International Icebowl, Belfast
Gary has represented Northern Ireland in the AMF World Cups in Brazil (1995) and Egypt (97), played for NI in the 1995 FIQ's (Reno) and the 1998 Commonwealth Games (Malaysia).
     
Top
   
 

Robert Walkingshaw

based in Dundonald International Icebowl, Belfast
Robert finished 2nd in the 1998-99 Rankings and has competed and won medals in two Junior Triple Crowns, concluding with a Gold Medal in the 1999 Under-18's Junior Triple Crown (Scotland). In 2000 Robert competed in his first adult Triple Crown (Leeds, England) and won Bronze in the Men's Doubles.
