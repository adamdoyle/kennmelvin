Bowling Books

Here's my thoughts on some books, not all directly concerned with bowling - but all of some relevance. Note that the NCF has many excellent books and other resources (including abstracts from periodicals). For a comprehensive listing of many other good books the USA Bowling Gold Workbook is a great resource too.

I also have reviews of some bowling videos.

  • Bowling: Knowledge is the Key
    Fred Borden     (ISBN 0-961919177-0-9,1986)
    Contains the knowledge of one of the sports most famous coaches. A definite buy for any serious coach. Keep an eye out for the revised edition (KITK for the year 2000).
  • Par Bowling, The Challenge
    Thomas Kouros     (ISBN ,1993)
    A truly excellent book, packed with in-depth writing. Some people seem to have a problem with Kouros and he does use a different terminology than most of us would be familiar with but. So, this book is not for everybody, but - if you are serious about your bowling and keen to further your knowledge to a deeper level - you should buy this (maybe even two copies, because if you lend it to a friend they may not want to give it back!).
  • Coaching
    Dave Chambers     (ISBN 1-55209-208-9,1997)
    Excellent book, applicable to any serious sport. Worth buying. Covers sport from planning the season, body composition, building strength, mental game, team building (including some good quotes) etc.
  • An Introduction to Sports Mechanics
    Karen Sprunt, NCF     (ISBN 0-947850-39-2,1992/1996)
    This "home study pack" provides an excellent introduction to biomechanics, including concepts such as forces, balance, and motion.
  • Dynamic Nutrition for Maximum Performance
    Daniel Gastelu & Dr. Fred Hatfield     (ISBN 0-89529-756-6, 1997)
    Split into four parts the book describes (in detail) nutrients, body composition, training and sport-specific plans for optimum performance (including bowling).

  • Revolutions II
    Chip Zielke (ISBN 0-9669011-0-X, 1998)
    A short and simple (almost a picture-book reader) book which explains, visually, the more modern concepts of cores, coverstocks and drillings affect the skid/hook and roll pattern of a ball. Recommended. Revolutions II is an excellent upgrade to the first edition providing a worldwide perspective of styles and conditions.
  • C.A.T.S. (Computer Aided Tracking System) Information Manual
    Dan Speranza, Larry Vezina
    Techie information on Cats data, lane conditions, ball drillings etc. Interesting charts showing correlation between ball weight and strikes, entry angle and ten-pin leaves etc.
  • Fitting and Drilling a Bowling Ball
    Bill, Taylor
    Apparently out of print and although quite old, this is, I believe, still a good reference book.

  • Mental Skills: An introduction for Sports Coaches
    Chris Sellars, NCF     (ISBN 0-947850-34-1, 1996)
    Available from the NCF (UK). An excellent introduction to the mental game, for coaches in any sport.
  • Self-Hypnosis
    Brian M. Alman & Peter Lambrou     (ISBN 0-87630-650-4,1983/1992)
    An easy to read yet comprehensive book which also includes a number of "situations" for which SH can be used, e.g. quitting smoking, increasing confidence, performance in sports etc.
  • Please Understand Me, Character & Temperment Types
    Kiersey & Bates     (ISBN 0-9606954-0-0, 1978)
    Einstein was feeling gloomy. A friend asked him, "What's the matter?" Einstein replied: "my wife just doesn't understand me!". Probably the most "popular" book on psychological temperment, i.e. a theory on why people are "different" and why it is important to recognise that.
  • Code of Ethics and Conduct for Sports Coaches
    The National Coaching Foundation     (ISBN 0-947850-34-1, 1996)
    Available from the NCF (UK), this booklet sets out a framework of standards and values to which coaches, in any sport, should adhere to.
