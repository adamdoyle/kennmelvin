Mental Game

From four to ninety-four people of all ages and abilities can play the game. Bowling is great in this respect and has one of the highest levels of participation of all sports across the world. Like other sports, though, as your technique gets better and you get more competitive the "Mental Game" becomes more important.

Let's think about the Mental Game for a second, look at these two pictures...

In the dieing seconds of a basketball game you are called on to shoot two free throws, your team is behind by one point and you can win the game
 

Do you see the concentration, the focus in these players eyes? Focus is important in this crunch situation: you are live on National TV, your team need you to make the shot and, so, the fans of the opposition will sit quietely while you can shoot the basket - Not! They will shout, stamp and wave brightly coloured objects specially designed to distract you.

So, What makes this situation what it is? Why do these guys make such big money when any kid in high school can make free throws? Just what's different here...

  • Pressure
  • Distractions
  • Noise
  • Adrenalin rush

Overcoming these factors is what makes winning so much sweeter - and losing so much more hard to take. So, what qualities do you need to succeed?

  • Confidence, in your own ability
  • Control, to manage the pressure
  • Concentration, to focus on the task in hand (not the distractions of the crowd
 These are three of "The Four-C's", the other being the Committment needed to reach high levels of your sport: while the never-give-up attitude does come into play during competition committment is more about the build up prior to competition. As much as some people think there is such thing as a free lunch and success does not come easy, it requires hours of practise, dedication and carefully designed training plans.
Summary
These intense high-pressure situations and stay-cool skills of high level athletes are not unique to Basketball. Indeed they are common to all sports. That's why bowling, like the other sports, now teaches The Mental Game as an essential part of your coaching programme.
