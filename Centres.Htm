Bowling Centres in Northern Ireland

There are several centres in Northern Ireland, all offering a broad range of amenities to both the customer and the more serious bowler.

  • Ballymena, "Ballymena Sportsbowl", 18 Brunswick lanes (synthetic)
  • Belfast, "Belfast Superbowl", 20 AMF lanes (wooden)
  • Belfast, "Dundonald International Icebowl", 30 AMF lanes (20 wooden, 10 guardian)
  • Coleraine, "Jet Centre", 18 Brunswick lanes (synthetic)
  • Glengormley, 18 Brunswick lanes (synthetic)
  • Londonderry, "Brunswick Superbowl", 20 Brunswick lanes (synthetic)
  • Newtownabbey, 16 Brunswick lanes (synthetic)
Revised June 19th 1999

Depending on where you are travelling from you can pick one or more maps to help plan your trip to our province. If you need any additional help please email me.


Flying

You can fly from many cities in England, Scotland and Europe into Belfast (City or International airports) or into L/Derry ("Eglinton"). Note that Belfast International is about 20 miles from the city centre.

Sailing

There are ferries into both Larne and Belfast harbours from Stranraer/Cairnryan (Scotland), Heysham (Liverpool) or Holyhead (Wales).

Driving

From Dublin take the N1 to Belfast (100m) or N2 to L/Derry (150m).
