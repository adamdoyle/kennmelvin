Links to bowling resources

In the early days of the web it was common, and useful, to provide links to other sites of relevance. The web has grown at a phenomenal rate, though, and now everybody and his dog can have a web site. There are many bowling sites, too, not all of them 'great', so I've been careful not to provide links to every site I've found: if you want that follow the first link in the list.

  • Tenpin Bowling All Over the Web the single best listing of bowling web sites.
  • Asian Bowling Tour, if you live in the west you should check this out
  • Asian Bowling Digest, informative web site worth a visit
  • Bowling This Month (BTM), the magazine for the serious bowler
  • Bowlers Journal International (BJI), more for the bowling proprietor, but web site has a good discussion board
  • Briggs Consulting Information on physical fitness programmes for bowlers
  • FIQ, Federation Internationale des Quilleurs, worldwide governing body for tenpin bowling
  • FIQ-EZ, good resource for European Bowling
  • The Foundation, doing great work to create challenging but fair lane conditions
  • Herbies, home of the excellent Kegel Training Center
  • PBA, Professional Bowling in the States
  • Strikeability, read and buy training material or consult the on-line "cyber coach"

Some other useful sites

Below you'll find a short list of sites, not directly related to bowling but which provide information that can be of use to a serious athlete or coach.

  • QuitNet - visit this site for information to help give up smoking
  • SportsCoach - a first class reference for coachs by Brian Mackenzie
  • Lawn Bowling in Australia: a different sport, but a great site - take the time to explore it, there's a lot of good material, including the mental game, biomechanics and considered opinions on the ups & downs of coaching.
  • Coaching Association of Canada
  • Australian Coaching Council
  • Biomechanics, links to many bio-mechanics web sites
  • NCF, The National Coaching Federation
  • Mind tools, excellent information on the mental game, worth checking out
  • Sport & Spine - Online therapy
